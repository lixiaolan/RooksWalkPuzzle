About Us

Seventh Harmonic was started by Lalit Jain, and Leland Jefferis, graduate students in math at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. They were later joined by Nathan Clement, fellow math grad student and artist. Seventh Harmonic's original goal was to draw a blue triangle right side up on a phone screen. One thing lead to another, and before long Seventh Harmonic became a proper business staged to launch their first product. Their new goal is to produce completely original mobile recreational software that looks great and plays smoothly.

Lalit Jain

Lalit's mathematical interests lie in Number Theory and Algebraic Geometry - areas he loves to talk about. In a former life, Lalit was a Teach for America teacher in San Francisco and he is still very passionate about educational reform. When not working on Bee-Line or his thesis (pretty much never) he enjoys playing video games, reading cheesy Sci-Fi and going hiking. His favorite functor is Hom.

Leland Jefferis

Leland is working towards a Ph.D. in computational mathematics for which he develops tricky numerical schemes for difficult physical simulations. He has more hobbies than are sustainable as a graduate student. These include surfing, ballroom dancing, music composition and foreign languages! Originally from the Pacific Northwest, he prefers a relaxed pace of life - something he remembers having 15 years ago. His favorite PDE is the Schrödinger equation with conical eigenenergy crossings. Here's his academic page.

Nathan Clement

Nathan Clement is a graduate student at the University of Wisconsin in the Math Ph.D. program. He enjoys tinkering with digital media when he has spare time, but Bee-Line is his first major project. As the project artist, Nathan would like to give a big shout out to the people behind the Inkscape program. He is Batman.
